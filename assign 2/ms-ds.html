MS (Data Science)

Objectives

Data Science has become increasingly important due to the need for analyzing and understanding the ever-increasing data generated by various sources. By getting admission in the MS programme, applicants will not only improve their qualifications but also be equipped with the latest knowledge in the field. Students will be exposed to different aspects of data science including programming, data structures, algorithms for data science, data analysis and visualization, and big data analytics. These aspects will provide students with opportunities to effectively work in local and international markets as well as to pursue a PhD in data science.

MS (Data Science) Part Time

Quaid-i-Azam University is keen to serve the community by offering an opportunity for individuals working in related fields who may not be able to attend a full-time post-graduate programme. Classes will be held in the evening after 5pm. By getting admission in the programme, applicants will not only improve their qualifications but will also be equipped with the latest knowledge and techniques in the field of information studies.

Programme Objectives

The main objectives of the programme are to enable students to:

  • Analyze and solve problems related to data for local and international markets.
  • Effectively use appropriate techniques and tools used in data science.
  • Learn state-of-the-art technologies associated with data science.
  • Pursue a PhD degree in data science or associated disciplines.

Eligibility

Students must have completed 16 years of education, either a BS (4 years) or MSc in computing-related disciplines.

Programme Structure

The MS programme consists of 30 credit hours. Each course is of 3 credit hours, and there are 2 compulsory courses along with a number of optional courses.

MS (Data Science) Programme Options

  • 24 credit hours for the course work and 6 credit hours for project/thesis.
  • 30 credit hours for the course work.

Deficiency Courses for Students from Non-Computing Backgrounds

Introduction to programming concepts and problem-solving strategies. Topics include data types, control structures, algorithms, and basic problem-solving techniques using programming languages.

CS 211: Data Structures

Introduction to data structures such as arrays, stacks, queues, linked lists, trees, and graphs. Emphasis on designing and implementing algorithms for efficient data manipulation.

Fundamentals of database design, relational model, SQL, normalization, and query optimization. Practical approaches to managing and using databases for real-world applications.
