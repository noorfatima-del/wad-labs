MPhil (Computer Science)

The objective of the MPhil Computer Science programme is to train students to initiate research work. The duration of the MPhil programme is two years (4 semesters).

MPhil is a 50 credit hour programme comprising 24 credit hours of course work and 26 credit hours of research work. Each course carries 3 credit hours. Students are required to complete 2 compulsory courses and choose 6 optional courses to fulfill the 24 credit hour coursework requirement. The courses are structured to expose students to research activities early in the programme, laying the foundation for their research work.

Research Component

Students will register for a research topic in the 3rd semester and are expected to complete their research work by the end of the 4th semester. The research will culminate in the submission of a thesis.

Compulsory course

CSC-702: Advanced Operating Systems

History and structure of Operating Systems; Inter-process communication (client-server, various flavors of RPC); Resource discovery, naming, and directory services; Group communication; Distributed synchronization (including distributed transactions); Scalable server design (including clusters); Distributed file systems; Distributed object systems; Distributed shared memory; Support for wide-area services; Support for mobile and disconnected services; Fault tolerance.

Optional Courses

CSC-601: Theory of Computation

Models of computations: partial recursive functions, Turing machines, alternative models of computing. Chomsky hierarchy of languages, Basic theory and limitations of computability. Reducibility & decidability, un-decidability, primitive & recursive functions, and Resource-bounded computational complexity, non-determinism, P, NP, NP-complete classes.

CSC-604: Advanced Software Engineering

Consideration and use of engineering principles to design and implement cost-effective, reliable software; Current software requirements methodologies and design practices, documentation standards, software project management, verification and validation techniques, software security considerations and computer human interfaces; Agent based software engineering; Clean-room process model; Management and evolution; costing and estimation; legacy systems.

CSC-611: Human and Information Interaction

Overview of the course and a brief history; Types and structures of information resources; Types and structures of vocabularies; Information Interaction in Search engines, Digital libraries; Search techniques and effectiveness; Advanced searching; Web search and the invisible web; Information seeking behaviour; User modelling; Mediation between search intermediaries and users; Evaluation of search sources and results; Result Presentation to users; Keeping up: sources for life-time learning.

CSC-782: Cloud Computing

Introduction to cloud computing; Cloud service models: SaaS, PaaS, IaaS; Virtualization and cloud architecture; Cloud deployment models: Private, Public, Hybrid; Cloud platforms and services; Cloud storage and computing; Cloud security and governance; Cloud scalability, elasticity, and fault tolerance; Service-level agreements in cloud computing.

CSC-783: Software Development for Mobile Platforms

Introduction to mobile application development; Mobile software development lifecycle; Design patterns for mobile apps; Mobile operating systems and platforms (Android, iOS); Mobile development tools and frameworks; User interface design for mobile platforms; Mobile performance and optimization; Mobile security; Deployment and testing for mobile apps.

CSC-784: Big Data Analytics

Introduction to Big Data; Data storage and retrieval in Big Data systems; Hadoop ecosystem; MapReduce programming model; Data processing frameworks for Big Data; NoSQL databases; Big Data tools and technologies; Real-time data processing; Data analytics methods; Machine learning applications on Big Data; Case studies of Big Data applications.

CSC-785: Information Security

Overview of information security; Principles of security: confidentiality, integrity, availability; Cryptography and encryption techniques; Secure network design and protocols; Security policies and risk management; Threats, vulnerabilities, and attacks; Intrusion detection systems; Security for distributed systems and cloud; Legal and ethical issues in information security.

CSC-786: Computer Networks

Overview of computer networks; OSI and TCP/IP models; Network topologies; Networking devices; Data transmission and protocols; Routing and switching; TCP, UDP, and their roles in networks; Network security and firewalls; Wireless networks and their protocols; Network management and monitoring; Advanced networking topics such as IPv6 and SDN.

CSC-787: High-Performance Computing

Introduction to high-performance computing (HPC); Parallel and distributed computing; Supercomputing architecture; High-performance algorithms; MPI and OpenMP for parallel programming; Performance optimization techniques; HPC applications; Cluster computing and grid computing; Cloud computing for high-performance applications.

CSC-788: Artificial Intelligence

Overview of Artificial Intelligence (AI); Intelligent agents; Search algorithms; Knowledge representation; Planning and reasoning; Machine learning: supervised, unsupervised, reinforcement learning; Natural language processing; Expert systems; AI applications: robotics, computer vision, autonomous systems.

CSC-789: Data Visualization

Introduction to data visualization; Principles of data visualization; Visual perception and cognition in design; Types of data visualizations: static, dynamic, interactive; Tools and software for data visualization; Data visualization techniques for large datasets; Case studies on data visualization in business, healthcare, and government sectors.

CSC-790: Knowledge Representation and Reasoning

Introduction to knowledge representation; Logical foundations of knowledge representation; Semantic networks, frames, and ontologies; Reasoning with uncertainty: fuzzy logic, probabilistic reasoning; First-order logic and its applications; Inference mechanisms; Applications of knowledge representation in AI systems.

CSC-791: Robotics

Introduction to robotics; Kinematics and dynamics of robotic systems; Sensors and actuators in robots; Robot programming and control; Autonomous robots; Mobile robots and navigation; Robot perception; Human-robot interaction; Robot learning and machine vision; Robotics applications in industry and research.

CSC-792: Computational Biology

Introduction to computational biology; Biological data analysis and modeling; Sequence alignment and bioinformatics tools; Computational genomics; Evolutionary models; Protein structure prediction; Bioinformatics algorithms and their applications; Computational methods for analyzing biological networks.

CSC-793: Quantum Computing

Introduction to quantum computing; Quantum mechanics fundamentals; Quantum gates and circuits; Quantum algorithms: Shor's algorithm, Grover's algorithm; Quantum computing models; Quantum programming languages; Applications of quantum computing; Quantum computing hardware and future trends.

CSC-794: Ethics in Computing

Overview of ethics in computing; Ethical issues in software development; Privacy and data protection in the digital age; Intellectual property rights and software piracy; Cybercrime and legal frameworks; Ethical decision-making in technology; Social impact of computing technologies; Case studies of ethical dilemmas in computing.

CSC-795: Advanced Topics in Computer Science

Topics to be announced; Emerging trends in computer science; Cutting-edge research in computer science; Case studies of recent advancements; Research methodologies and future directions; Special projects and seminars on advanced topics in computer science.

CSC-796: Independent Research

Research methodology and techniques; Independent research project under faculty supervision; Literature review; Hypothesis formulation; Data collection and analysis; Writing and presenting research findings; Preparing for academic publication.

CSC-797: Thesis

Overview of the thesis process; Thesis proposal writing and approval; Research objectives and methodology; Thesis writing and structure; Data analysis and interpretation; Preparing for thesis defense; Submission and publication of the thesis.
