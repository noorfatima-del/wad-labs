BS COMPUTER SCIENCE

The objective of the BS-COMPUTER SCIENCE programme is to train students to initiate research work. The duration of the BSCS programme is four years (8 semesters).

BS (Computer Science)

Introduction

The BS Computer Science program at QAU was started in 2013. It provides a well-integrated balance of theoretical and practical knowledge required for developing reliable and usable software tools for different fields. The program enables students to design, implement and manage systems combining scientific, computational, communication, and multimedia facilities in different domains. The BS program is accredited by the National Computing Education Accreditation Council (NCEAC).

Program Educational Objectives

  • PEO1: to use their computing knowledge for developing and maintaining software using modern tools and technologies
  • PEO2: to benefit the society as responsible Computer Science professionals by practically applying their knowledge in diverse areas
  • PEO3: to keep abreast of latest technological developments, participate in lifelong learning and progress in their chosen profession

BS Program Structure

During the B.S. students will be informed through research expertise of the faculty about the current and future issues affecting the usage and development of computer-based systems. Initially, students learn the basics of computing discipline through foundation courses and will build their knowledge of the discipline through core courses. In the last four semesters, students will extend their knowledge in a focus area of their choice through elective courses. The final year compulsory project will give students the opportunity to consolidate and demonstrate the knowledge and skills gained from the entire course material.

Focus Areas

After providing the students with a solid foundation based on foundation and core courses in Computing, the BS program will give students the opportunity to enrich their knowledge in an area through elective courses. These elective courses will on one hand provide in-depth knowledge, and on the other hand, will enable students to work on and solve practical problems in the area by using the latest techniques and tools. Some focus areas are:

  • Artificial Intelligence
  • Data Science
  • Software Engineering
  • Human Centered Computing
  • Information Systems
  • Networks and Security

Courses Overview

Four years of BS program is divided into eight semesters. In each semester, students are offered 15 to 18 credit hours of courses, where one credit hour means one hour of teaching (or three hours of lab) per week. The students have to complete 130 credit hours during their degree. The courses are divided into six groups:

  • Computing Core courses
  • Domain Core courses
  • Domain Elective courses
  • Maths and Supporting courses
  • Elective Supporting courses
  • General Education courses
